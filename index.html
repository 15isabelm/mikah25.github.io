HIDDEN IN PLAIN SIGHT

Tracking the routes, crimes, and costs of the illegal horn and tusk trade.

It can be hard to grasp the scale and impact of wildlife trafficking when it feels so far removed from daily life. But this illegal trade isn’t just a distant crisis—it’s deeply connected to the ways we value life, profit, and protection. And this is where the story begins, with the life of a single rhino.

Early Years

Newborn rhino calves and their mothers bear a special bond. With females only having one offspring per pregnancy, they are take devoted care of their children with calves often staying with their mothers for up to 4 years after birth.

Year 3

The Encounter

Suddenly, in a fleeting moment when the calf drifts too far, a loud crack echoes across the land.

With fear rising, he makes his way back to his mother, only to find her mutilated corpse lying in the silt.

In as fast as 10 minutes, a group of poachers took the rhino mother’s innocent life, and made off with its horn.
